Projects
Prosthetic Knee with Standup Support Scope: Private Project Wearers of knee prostheses, especially among the elderly, often report difficulties when sitting down and standing up. Providing the required force with only one leg is perceived as uncomfortable and unsafe. This knee prosthesis provides support when sitting down, stores the energy, and releases it to assist when standing up. It is a purely mechanical system that does not rely on any external energy sources. Focus is on simple handling and absolute safety.
AR Assistance for Laparoscopic Surgery Scope: University Project at TUM Laparoscopic surgery is a demanding task that is often associated with unergonomic and uncomfortable working postures. Augmented Reality (AR) can assist both for educational purposes and during actual surgery. This application for Microsoft HoloLens generates a virtual window into the patient, through which the surgeon can see anatomical structures and the current tool position. Furthermore, the camera view from the laparoscope is cast to a virtual screen. The screen adapts to the surgeon's pose and view angle, reducing the need for unforfortable working positions.
Tactile Sensor for Prosthetic Hand Scope: University Project at TUM Tactile sensing in prosthetics enables advanced possibilities for patients to perceive and interact with their environment. This sensor has been developed for the prosthetic hand developed by TUM for the Cybathlon, a prosthetics competition held by ETH Zurich. The development process included reasearch into various sensor principles, as well as building two prototypes, one relying on magnetic and the other on resistive sensing.
Control System for Partial Hand Prosthesis Scope: Research Assistance Project at TUM Highly individualized hand prosthesis for a patient with symbrachydactyly. The small and lightweight control device is carried in a bracelet around the wrist. It contains the electronics required to power and control the hand prosthesis: a microcontroller, motor drivers, power electronics and sensor electronics for processing input signals.
Training Device for Stroke Rehabilitation Scope: Think.Make.Start. at TUM In consequence of a stroke, brain damage can lead to partial paralysis. In spite of this severe condition, patients can relearn the ability to move by undergoing long and tedious therapy. The initial training progress is extremely slow and barely noticeable. This lack of perceived success leads to frustration, and many patients abandon their therapy. This device amplifies and records muscle signals during training in order to make them visible, provide a significant boost in motivation, and allow for progress tracking during rehabilitation.
Model Predictive Control Simulation Scope: Private Project Implementation of a Linear Model Predictive Controller (MPC) based on the OSQP solver. The MPC is used to generate control sequences for a simple physical model of a quadcopter. The model itself is nonlinear, but the controller uses a linearized representation.
